## 📋 Scholarship Programs

### Central Carolina Promise (K14)

- **What it covers:** Up to **two years** of free in-state tuition and required fees.
- **Who qualifies:** Recent graduates of public high schools, private schools, charter schools, or homeschools who reside in **Chatham, Harnett, or Lee counties**.
- **Application:** Students must complete the [Foundation Scholarship Application](https://www.cccc.edu/scholarships).

### Chatham Adult Promise

- **Status:** Coming soon.
- **What it covers:** Up to **$5,500** for tuition and fees.
- **Who qualifies:** Chatham County residents who do **not** have a college degree and are **not** eligible for the K14 program.
- **Application:** Students must complete the [Foundation Scholarship Application](https://www.cccc.edu/scholarships) when available.

### General Foundation Scholarships

The Foundation oversees numerous individual scholarships with varying criteria, including:

- Academic achievement
- Financial need
- Specific programs of study

**Example:** The *Howard-James Scholarship* is designated for students in CTE programs.

Students should apply through the [Foundation Scholarship Application](https://www.cccc.edu/scholarships) and review available scholarships at [cccc.edu/foundation](https://www.cccc.edu/foundation/).

## 🎓 Special Assistance: Graduation Regalia

If a student expresses concern about the cost of **graduation regalia** (cap and gown), the Foundation may be able to provide emergency assistance or scholarship funds to cover these expenses.

> **Staff Action:** Instruct the student to reach out to the Foundation office (Abby Walker) **as early as possible** to request support. Do not wait until close to the ceremony — funds may be limited.

## 🖥️ Internal Staff Workflow

| Step | Details |
|---|---|
| **Application** | Students must complete a separate [Foundation Scholarship Application](https://www.cccc.edu/scholarships). This is distinct from the FAFSA. |
| **FAFSA** | The Foundation often uses FAFSA data to determine financial need — confirm the student has completed it before referring for need-based scholarships. |
| **Disbursement** | Once awarded, scholarship funds are coordinated through the Financial Aid office for disbursement to the student's account. |
| **Checking Status** | To verify if a scholarship has been applied to a student's record, check the **ASUM** or **SASM** screens in Colleague. |

### Referral Triage Questions

Before referring a student to the Foundation, clarify:

1. Is the student a recent high school graduate from Chatham, Harnett, or Lee County? *(Determines K14 eligibility.)*
2. Has the student already completed the FAFSA? *(Required for need-based awards.)*
3. Is the student looking for help with **tuition**, or specifically for **graduation regalia costs**?
