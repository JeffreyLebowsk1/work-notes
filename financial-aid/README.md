## 📝 FAFSA & Core Eligibility

- **Application URL:** [studentaid.gov](https://studentaid.gov)
- **CCCC School Code:** **005449**

### Core Requirements for Aid Eligibility

Students must meet **all** of the following to receive federal or state financial aid:

| Requirement | Details |
|---|---|
| Citizenship | U.S. citizen or eligible non-citizen |
| Social Security Number | Valid SSN on record with CCCC |
| High School Credential | Official high school transcript or GED/HSE on file |
| Enrolled Program | Must be in an eligible degree or certificate program |

### Special Credit Ineligibility

> ⚠️ Students enrolled under the **Special Credit (T90990)** code are **not** eligible for federal or state financial aid.

### Retake Policy

- Financial Aid will pay for a student to repeat a **passed course** (grade of D or better) **one additional time only**.
- Aid will **not** pay to retake a course that was **transferred in** from another institution to receive a higher grade.

## 📊 2026–2027 Pell Grant & FAFSA Updates (OBBBA)

Per the *One Big Beautiful Bill Act* (OBBBA), the following changes take effect for the **2026–2027** award year:

| Change | Details |
|---|---|
| **Asset Exclusions** | The Student Aid Index (SAI) now excludes the net worth of: family-owned businesses (≤100 employees), farms on which the family resides, and family-controlled commercial fishing businesses. |
| **Pell Ineligibility Threshold** | Applicants with an SAI of **$14,790 or higher** (twice the maximum Pell award) are ineligible for a Pell Grant, unless they qualify under specific rules for dependents of deceased servicemembers or Public Safety Officers. |
| **Foreign Income** | Foreign earned income exclusions must now be **added back to AGI** when determining eligibility. |
| **Workforce Pell** | Beginning **July 1, 2026**, students in select Continuing Education programs (150–600 clock hours) may become eligible for Pell funding. See also: [Continuing Education](../continuing-education/). |

> See also: [OBBBA Policy Update entry in policy-updates.md](../updates/policy-updates.md)

## 📈 Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP)

To maintain financial aid eligibility, students must meet all three SAP standards each semester. SAP is evaluated by the Financial Aid Office at the end of every term (Fall, Spring, and Summer). All periods of enrollment are included in this evaluation, regardless of whether aid was received during that term.

| Standard | Requirement |
|---|---|
| **Qualitative (GPA)** | Minimum **2.0 cumulative GPA** |
| **Quantitative (Pace)** | Successfully complete at least **67%** of all cumulative credit hours attempted |
| **Maximum Timeframe** | Complete their program within **150%** of the published length (e.g., 90 attempted hours for a 60-hour program) |

### Critical Rules & Limitations

**Withdrawals and Incompletes (W, WF, I, F, R)**
Grades of **W, WF, I, F, and R** count as **attempted hours but not completed hours**, which lowers a student's PACE completion rate and counts toward the 150% Maximum Timeframe. Specifically:
- A **W** or **WF** lowers the completion rate but does not affect the academic GPA.
- An **I (Incomplete)** is treated the same as a withdrawal for PACE purposes until a passing final grade replaces it.
- If a student is considering a withdrawal, you can estimate the impact by adding the current course credits to their "Attempted" total while keeping their "Completed" total unchanged.

**Transfer Credits**
All hours transferred and accepted from other institutions are counted as both **attempted and completed** in the PACE calculation.

**Developmental Coursework**
Aid is limited to **30 credit hours** of remedial/developmental courses. These courses are included in the PACE calculation.

**Two-Degree Limit**
Financial Aid will only pay for a maximum of **two associate degrees** at CCCC.

**Graduation Impact**
Once a student graduates from any program, they are automatically placed on **"Maximum Timeframe"** status. They must submit a SAP Appeal before aid can be reinstated for a second program.

### SAP Eligibility Status Flow

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Satisfactory** | The student is meeting all SAP standards and aid continues normally. |
| **Warning** | The student failed to meet the GPA or PACE standard for one term. Aid **can still be received** during this one-term warning period. |
| **Suspension** | The student failed to meet standards after the warning term, or failed for two consecutive terms. Aid eligibility is **lost immediately**. |
| **Probation** | Granted only after a successful SAP Appeal. Aid is reinstated for **one term**. The student must meet SAP standards or follow their specific academic progress plan to continue receiving aid. |

### SAP Suspension & Regaining Eligibility

If a student is placed on **Financial Aid Suspension**, they have two options:
1. **Pay out-of-pocket** until they meet SAP standards.
2. **Submit a SAP Appeal** documenting extenuating circumstances (e.g., illness, death in family, military deployment). See [Maximum Timeframe — Detail & Appeals](#-maximum-timeframe--detail--appeals) for the full appeal process and deadlines.

> **Terminology Note:** The 2025-2026 Catalog uses the term "Academic Probation" with a two-semester threshold before suspension. Per administrative updates ("All Notes - Matt"), the correct term is **"Academic Warning,"** and the threshold is **three consecutive semesters** of a GPA below 2.0. See [policy-updates.md](../updates/policy-updates.md) for the full entry. This conflict is tracked in [conflicts-pending.md](../updates/conflicts-pending.md) — do not use "Academic Probation" in student communications.

For a detailed breakdown of grades and their SAP impact, see [Academic Advising Policy Manual — Section 4](../updates/academic-advising-policy-2025-2026.md).

### Talking Points for Students

- *"To keep your aid, you need to maintain at least a 2.0 GPA and pass 67% of your classes. Withdrawing from a class might not hurt your academic GPA, but it does count against your financial aid completion rate."*
- *"An incomplete grade will lower your completion rate just like a withdrawal until you finish the work and earn a final grade."*
- *"Every class you withdraw from uses up part of your 150% maximum timeframe. If you have a 60-credit degree, federal rules only allow us to pay for 90 attempted credits in total."*
- *"Keep in mind that financial aid only covers up to two associate degrees here at CCCC."*
- *"If you've already graduated from a program, you'll need to file an appeal with the Financial Aid Office before you can receive money for a new major."*

## 📊 Maximum Timeframe — Detail & Appeals

Once a student attempts 150% of the credit hours required for their program (e.g., 90 attempted hours for a 60-hour degree), they become ineligible for federal, state, and institutional financial aid.

### What Counts Toward the 150% Limit

- All attempted credits from **every period of enrollment** at CCCC, including grades of W, WF, I, F, and R.
- All credits **transferred in** from other institutions.
- Any student who **graduates** from a degree or program is automatically placed on Maximum Timeframe status and must appeal to receive aid for a second program.
- Financial aid will only cover a maximum of **two associate degrees** at CCCC.

### The Appeal Process

Students may regain eligibility only through an approved SAP Appeal:

1. **Submit the Form:** Complete the **Satisfactory Academic Progress Appeal Request** form (available from the Financial Aid Office).
2. **Personal Statement:** Provide a written statement explaining the extenuating circumstances that affected progress and what has changed to allow timely graduation.
3. **Third-Party Documentation:** Include supporting evidence such as medical records, accident reports, or death certificates.
4. **Submit on Time:** Incomplete submissions (missing personal statement or documentation) will not be evaluated.

### SAP Appeal Deadlines — 2025–2026

Appeals submitted after the deadline will be reviewed for the **subsequent** semester.

| Semester | Appeal Deadline |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Fall** | July 1 |
| **Spring** | November 1 |
| **Summer** | April 1 |

If an appeal is approved, the student is placed on **Probation** and is eligible for aid for one term. They must then meet SAP standards or follow their specific academic progress plan to continue.

### Talking Points for Students

- *"Because you've reached the 150% credit limit for your program, federal regulations require us to pause your financial aid. This limit includes every class you've attempted here and any credits you've transferred from other colleges."*
- *"If you have already graduated from one program, you're automatically placed on this status. To get aid for a new major, you'll need to submit an appeal to the Financial Aid Office."*
- *"The appeal process allows you to explain any extenuating circumstances, like an illness or family emergency, that caused you to take extra credits. You'll need to provide documentation to support your request."*
- *"If you need to appeal your financial aid suspension, the deadline for the **Fall** semester is **July 1**. If you miss this date, your appeal won't be reviewed until the next term."*
- *"Make sure your appeal is complete before submitting it. You'll need a personal statement explaining what happened and documentation from a third party, like a doctor or employer, to support your claim."*
- *"If your appeal is approved, you will be on probation. You'll need to follow your specific success plan to keep your aid for future semesters."*
