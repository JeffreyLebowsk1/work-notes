# 🏛️ North Carolina Residency Determination Service (NC RDS)

> **Reference only.** Always verify current policies and procedures at [ncresidency.org](https://www.ncresidency.org) or with your supervisor.

The NC Residency Determination Service (RDS) is the **single, centralized statewide authority** for determining a student's residency status for in-state tuition and state financial aid eligibility at all North Carolina public universities (UNC System), community colleges (NCCCS), and private institutions (NCICU). It is administered by the **NC State Education Assistance Authority (NCSEAA)**.

Centralization guarantees that an applicant's residency classification is **identical regardless of which NC institution they attend** — eliminating inconsistencies and "system shopping."

---

## 🔑 The Three-Part Legal Standard (Domicile)

To qualify for in-state tuition or state grants, a student must satisfy **all three** of the following:

| Requirement | What It Means |
|---|---|
| **Established Legal Domicile** | Permanent legal residence in NC — the place the student intends to return to whenever absent. |
| **12-Month Presence** | Domicile maintained for a minimum of **12 consecutive months** immediately before the date residency is claimed. |
| **Intent for Permanence** | The student must intend to make NC a permanent home **indefinitely** — not merely relocating to attend college. Verified by civil acts (tax filings, vehicle/voter registration, etc.). |

> ⚠️ Any applicant who has not yet maintained NC domicile for a full 12 consecutive months will receive an **initial out-of-state determination**.

> The "intent indefinitely" clause targets **tuition shoppers** — individuals seeking lower rates without genuine long-term commitment.

---

## 📋 Required Documentation / Inputs for the Online Interview

Students should gather the following **before** starting the RDS online interview at [ncresidency.org](https://www.ncresidency.org):

- Driver's License or Non-Driver ID
- Tax Return information
- Vehicle License and Registration information
- Social Security Number (SSN)
- Voter Registration (if applicable)
- Immigration documents, if applicable:
  - Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN)
  - Alien Registration Number (ARN / A-number)
  - USCIS number

> ⚠️ **Critical:** If an applicant cannot or will not provide a required identification number (SSN, ITIN, ARN, etc.), RDS **cannot** validate their claim electronically and is required by NC statute to classify the student as a **non-resident** for both tuition and state financial aid. See the [RDS Guidebook](https://www.ncresidency.org) for the specific statutory citation.

## 🆔 The Residency Certification Number (RCN)

The **RCN** is the unique identifier issued to a student upon completion of the RDS determination. It is the cornerstone of how the centralized system integrates with individual campuses.

### RCN Operational Flow

| Step | Responsible Party | Action / Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| Determination Completion | Student | Receives validated residency decision and unique RCN from RDS. |
| RCN Submission | Student | Must provide the RCN to **every** college/university they are applying to. |
| Data Download | Campus / Institution | Uses RCN to securely retrieve official residency status data from RDS. |
| Billing Default | Campus / Institution | **Bills the student as a non-resident if no valid RCN is provided.** |

### Key Points
- The RCN should be treated as **mandatory admissions documentation** — equivalent to transcripts or test scores.
- The non-resident billing default is the enforcement mechanism that drives timely RCN submission.
- If a determination changes (via appeal or reconsideration), the **student is responsible** for providing the updated RCN to each campus and requesting that the institution pull the new data.

| Organization | Role |
|---|---|
| **NCSEAA** | Administers and manages NC residency law through RDS. |
| **UNC System** | 4-year public institutions — uses RDS determination for tuition. |

| **NCICU** | Private colleges — uses RDS determination for **state financial aid eligibility** (not subsidized tuition). |

## 🔗 Interagency Data Validation Network

RDS cross-checks applicant claims electronically against a wide network of state and federal databases. This is how the subjective "intent" element is verified objectively.

### NC State Agencies

| Agency | Data Verified |
|---|---|
| NC Department of Revenue | State tax filing history; declared residency for tax purposes |
| NC Department of Transportation (DOT) | Driver's license, non-driver ID, vehicle registration |
| NC State Board of Elections | Voter registration status |
| NC Department of Commerce / DES | Employment records, payroll ties |
| NC Department of Health and Human Services (DHHS) | Health and human services records confirming residential presence |
| NC DPI & NC Grant System | Prior K-12 history; previous state financial aid applications |

### Federal and Third-Party

| Entity | Data Verified |
|---|---|
| US DHS / USCIS | Immigration documents, ITINs, Alien Registration Numbers |
| US Department of Defense (DOD) | Military service and dependent status for statutory exceptions |
| National Student Clearinghouse | Enrollment history at other institutions |
| Third-party data aggregators | Supplemental background data checks |

## ⚖️ Exceptions and Special Circumstances

### Military and Veterans
- Active duty military, veterans, and their dependents may receive **in-state billing adjustments** even if RDS issues an initial non-resident determination.
- These adjustments are processed through the campus **Military & Veteran Services** office — the standard 12-month durational requirement does not penalize families on temporary military assignment.

### Graduate Students
- Graduate applicants claiming NC residency must complete the RDS determination at [ncresidency.org](https://www.ncresidency.org) and provide the RCN with their application.
- Failure to provide the RCN = classified as **out-of-state** until the RCN is submitted and processed.

---

## 📣 Appeals and Reconsideration

All students have the **right to appeal** a non-resident determination. The process is two-tiered:

1. **Reconsideration** — Student submits alternative hard documentation to support their residency claim (used when electronic validation could not be completed, e.g., no SSN/ITIN provided).
2. **Formal Appeal** — If reconsideration is denied, a formal appeal may be filed per the RDS Guidebook requirements.

> See the [RDS Guidebook at ncresidency.org](https://www.ncresidency.org) for submission requirements.

## 🔒 Data Security Infrastructure

Given the sensitivity of data exchanged (SSNs, tax records, immigration status), RDS employs a **Defense in Depth** security model:

| Safeguard | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **Firewalls** | Block unauthorized external access to the RDS network. |
| **SSL Encryption** | Protects data in transit between the applicant's browser, RDS servers, and partner agency servers. |
| **Anti-virus / Malware Protection** | Detects and prevents malicious intrusions into network systems. |

---

## 💡 Key Administrative Reminders

1. **RCN = mandatory.** Communicate to students that not submitting the RCN results in automatic non-resident billing — financial consequence is immediate.
2. **RDS is the sole authority.** Do not attempt to override or independently adjudicate residency outside of the RDS process (except military adjustments through Veteran Services).
3. **State aid scope.** RDS eligibility affects state grants at *all* NC institutions, including private colleges — not just public school tuition.
4. **Student responsibility for updates.** After any appeal or reconsideration, the student must re-submit the updated RCN to each campus.
