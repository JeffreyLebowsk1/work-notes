## Military Students

- If a student is **out-of-state/country due to military parent(s)**, provide a copy of the parent's military status papers to RDS to push through and grant in-state tuition.
- If a student is **currently active duty and does not PCS until after the semester starts**, they can complete the Military Waiver to receive in-state tuition for that semester.
- If the student **PCS's at application time**, they have **30 days** to update:
  - Home of record with the VA
  - Driver's license
  - Vehicle registration
  - Voter registration
  - Leases
  - Other applicable documents
  
  Then provide that documentation to NC RDS for reclassification → in-state tuition granted; **365-day waiting period is waived**.

## Reconsideration

**Reconsideration** is what a student needs when they want to update their information with RDS (e.g., change in personal/living situation, correcting errors, submitting missing documents).

Students go to [www.NCresidency.org](https://www.ncresidency.org), log in, and request reconsideration.

**Complete reconsideration if:**
- Incomplete information or missing data
- Failure to submit required documents on time
- Error while entering information
- Change in personal/living situation

> Students who are **continuously enrolled** at the same academic level do not need reconsideration when applying to or changing programs — unless the classification is rebutted by new information.

## Reconsideration & Appeals Process

A student may request an appeal **within 10 calendar days** of their determination. After requesting the appeal, the student then has **25 days** to submit the full request with all supporting documentation.

### 1. Reconsideration
- Allows student to change info about themselves, parents/legal guardians, and/or spouse
- Go to [NCresidency.org](https://www.ncresidency.org) and log in

### 2. RDS Appeal
- Must be requested online within 10 calendar days of determination
- After requesting, submit additional information and documentation within 25 days
- Both the additional documentation and the appeal request form must be submitted within those 25 days
- Answer calls, emails, and/or meet face-to-face to answer questions and review the appeal

### 3. SEAA Appeal
- Final administrative step in the residency process
- Cannot be completed before the RDS Appeal
- No additional documentation or information is permitted
- Conducted by a statewide committee comprised of representatives from:
  - [NC Community College System](https://www.nccommunitycolleges.edu)
  - [NC Independent Colleges and Universities](https://ncicu.org)
  - [State Education Assistance Authority (SEAA)](https://www.ncseaa.edu)
  - University of North Carolina System

## Purpose & Background

In 2013, the NC General Assembly directed UNC General Administration, the [NC Community College System (NCCCS)](https://www.nccommunitycolleges.edu), the [NC State Education Assistance Authority (SEAA)](https://www.ncseaa.edu), and the [NC Independent Colleges and Universities (NCICU)](https://ncicu.org) to create a centralized, uniform process for determining residency for tuition purposes and for administration of state financial aid.

The state of North Carolina partially subsidizes the cost of tuition for students whose domicile (permanent legal residence) is in North Carolina. Article IX, Section 9, of the State Constitution states:

> *"The General Assembly shall provide that the benefits of the University of North Carolina and other public institutions of higher education, as far as practicable, be extended to the people of the State free of expense."*

In-state tuition is considered a **taxpayer benefit**; out-of-state students are welcome but pay full rates.

### Requirements for In-State Tuition

To qualify for in-state tuition, a student must show that:
1. They have established their legal residence (domicile) in North Carolina
2. They have maintained that domicile for at least **12 consecutive months** before the beginning of the term
3. They have a residentiary presence in the state
4. They intend to make North Carolina their **permanent home indefinitely** (not solely to attend college)

### Factors Considered for Domiciliary Intent

No single factor is conclusive. RDS evaluates:
- Whether the student lives in their parent's home
- Where they are permanently employed
- Where they are registered to vote
- Sources of financial support
- Where their vehicle is registered
- Which state issued their driver's license or state ID
- Where they own a home or real estate serving as primary residence
- Where they filed state income tax returns
- Where they last attended high school

### Transfer Students

Students who transfer between institutions (at any level) or apply to a different academic level at the same institution must **reapply via RDS** to receive in-state tuition.
