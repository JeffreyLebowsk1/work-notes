# GED® vs. HiSET® — Staff Reference

Quick reference for advising students on North Carolina High School Equivalency (HSE) test options.

---

## Key Differences

| Feature | GED® | HiSET® |
|---|---|---|
| **Number of tests** | 4 | 5 |
| **Testing modality** | Computer-based only | Computer-based **or** paper-based |

---

## Key Similarities

| Feature | Both Tests |
|---|---|
| **Content** | Same core academic content areas — demonstrates proficiency equivalent to a high school graduate |
| **Credential issued** | North Carolina High School Equivalency Diploma, issued by the NC State Board of Community Colleges |
| **Record ordering** | Official transcripts and diplomas managed exclusively through **DiplomaSender** |
| **Direct delivery** | Must be sent directly to CCCC at **admissions@cccc.edu** to be considered official |
| **Cost for records** | **$20.00 per document** — see conflict note below |

### ⚠️ Fee Conflict Note

Some internal notes reference a fee of **$15.00** through DiplomaSender. Official policy requires **$20.00 per document**. Always verify current pricing on the [CCCC Transcript Request page](https://www.cccc.edu/support-success/all-offices-services/student-records-and-registrars-office/cccc-transcript-request) before confirming with a student. See also: [Conflicts Pending — CONFLICT 4](../updates/conflicts-pending.md).

---

## Staff Talking Points

- *"Both tests lead to the exact same North Carolina High School Equivalency Diploma."*
- *"If you prefer paper-and-pencil testing, the HiSET® is the only option that offers that format."*
- *"The GED® has one fewer test section than the HiSET®, but it must be taken on a computer."*
- *"Classes to help you prepare for these tests are free at CCCC."*
