# 🏠 Homeschool Transcript Requirements

> **Staff reference** for processing homeschool applicants. Covers required documentation, transcript content standards, testing requirements, communication templates, and Colleague entry procedures.

---

## 1. Required Registration Documentation

All homeschool applicants must submit the following alongside their transcript:

- **Proof of DNPE Listing:** Proof of listing with the **N.C. Division of Non-Public Education (DNPE)**.
- **Certification Card:** A copy of the **NCDNPE homeschool certification or registration card**.

---

## 2. Transcript Content Requirements

The transcript must be a full, final record and include all of the following:

| Field | Requirement |
|---|---|
| **School Header** | School name, address, phone number, contact email, and name of administrator |
| **Coursework Details** | All courses taken (completed, in-progress, and planned), preferably broken down by grade level and academic year |
| **Grades and Scale** | Final course grades and a clear listing of the grade scale used (e.g., A = 90–100) |
| **Unweighted GPA** | Cumulative unweighted GPA on a 4.0 scale — weighted GPAs **cannot** be used for English or Math course placement |
| **Exact Graduation Date** | Graduation date in **mm/dd/yyyy** format — transcripts listing only month/year or that are future-dated will **not** be processed as official |
| **Administrator Signature** | Transcript must be signed and dated by the homeschool administrator |

---

## 3. Standardized Testing Requirements

- **General Rule:** Documentation of nationally standardized test scores for the **last two years** of home school instruction is required.
- **COVID-19 Exception:** For students who were juniors and seniors during the **2019–2021** academic years, only **one year** of standardized testing results is required.

---

## 4. Important Staff Reminders

- **Official Submission via Email:** CCCC's standard policy is that emailed transcripts are considered unofficial. Homeschool transcripts are the **only exception** — they **can** be accepted as official when received via email, specifically for homeschool students.
- **Financial Aid Impact:** While a missing high school transcript does not prevent a student from enrolling in classes (unless they are a CCP student), it **will prevent financial aid from being awarded** and prevent the college from awarding a degree.
- **HSE Alternative:** Homeschooled students may elect to take the **High School Equivalency (HSE)** exam; if passed, the HSE transcript can replace the high school diploma requirement.
- **Missing Graduation Date:** If the student's transcript is missing the graduation date, we can accept an emailed letter from the school stating the month and year of graduation, though the full date on the transcript is preferred.

---

## 5. Communication Guidance

### 5.1 If Helping by Phone

Use the following talking points when a homeschool applicant calls to ask about submitting their transcript:

- **Yes, you can email it.** You can send your homeschool transcript directly to our admissions email at admissions@cccc.edu.
- **Check the details.** To be considered official, your transcript must include your exact graduation date (mm/dd/yyyy), your unweighted GPA on a 4.0 scale, and the administrator's signature.
- **Don't forget the DNPE proof.** We also need a copy of your homeschool certification or registration card from the N.C. Division of Non-Public Education (DNPE).
- **Financial Aid warning.** While you can register for classes without it, we cannot award a degree or finalize your financial aid until this official record is on file.

## 6. Internal Staff Actions

After receiving a homeschool transcript, complete the following steps in Colleague:

### 6.1 Check Colleague — XRDS Screen

Use the **XRDS** screen to verify the student's **RCN (Residency Certification Number)** and current residency status before processing the transcript.

### 6.2 HSA Entry — IASU >> HSA Screen

When the transcript arrives, enter the data on the **IASU >> HSA** screen in Colleague:

- If the transcript provides an unusable GPA or only a weighted GPA, enter **0.111** on the **HSA** and **TSUM** screens. This sentinel value satisfies the system's admission data requirement while flagging the record for further manual review (a real GPA will never be 0.111, making it easy to identify records needing follow-up).
- Verify all data entry on the **SPRO** and **HSA** screens once the document is received and entered.

### 6.3 Financial Aid Reminder

Remind the student that while they can register for classes without a final transcript on file, **Financial Aid cannot award or disburse funds** until the official record is received and verified. The student's aid package will remain pending until the transcript is fully processed.

### 6.4 HSE / GED Alternative

If the student cannot provide a transcript, they may elect to take the **High School Equivalency (HSE)** exam, which can replace the diploma requirement.
