## Foreign Students

- Put a **CKDOC** doc hold in SPRO for foreign students.
- They **cannot** be added to any Allied Health program.
- You can put them in AA, AS, or T90990 to prevent them from receiving FA.
- They can provide documents in person.

> If a student attended a foreign high school, their transcript(s) must be evaluated and translated (if applicable) by a current member of the **National Association of Credential Evaluation Services** ([naces.org](https://www.naces.org)) or a current member of the **Association of International Credential Evaluators** ([aice-eval.org](https://www.aice-eval.org)). Completed evaluations, along with copies of the original transcript(s), should be sent directly from the evaluation agency to CCCC Admissions. If the evaluation is sent directly to the student, it must remain in the original sealed envelope to be considered official. Once opened, it is no longer official.

## Foreign Nationals

- A Foreign National **can** enter Allied Health Programs **only** if they have a **valid green card / permanent residency card**.
- J-1 or J-2 visas (documented non-immigrant and/or a dependent of) are **not** permanent residents and cannot be used to enter a program — they only confirm the person is here legally, not permanently.
- Must be a U.S. citizen to pass background checks and to take/pass state board exams and obtain state certifications.
- Consult the spreadsheet for more details regarding foreign nationals and limited enrollment programs.
- Policy references: NCCCS CC-13-019, 8 U.S.C. § 1621(a) and (c)(1)(A), SBCC Code 1D SBCCC 400.2(b)(5)) — see top right of page 11 in the current College Catalog.

### J-1 and J-2 Visa Holders — Key Facts

Individuals on J-1 and J-2 visas are considered **documented nonimmigrants** — they have legal authorization to be in the U.S. for a specific purpose and duration, but they are **not** permanent residents.

| Category | J-1 | J-2 |
|---|---|---|
| Who | Exchange visitors (research, medical training, cultural exchanges) | Dependents (spouse or children under 21) of J-1 holders |
| Work Authorization | May work depending on program terms | Can apply for EAD but cannot work to support the J-1 holder |
| Study Eligibility | May study without additional student visa | May study without additional student visa |
| Permanent Residency | No — unless status is adjusted via another visa | No — unless status is adjusted via another visa |

> Some J-1 holders (and J-2 dependents) are subject to the **two-year home residency requirement** — they must return to their home country for two years before applying for certain U.S. visas or permanent residency.

## Out-of-State Nurse Aides

Out-of-state nurse aides may be able to be listed on the **NC Nurse Aide I Registry** via one of two pathways:

### Training Waiver

- Must have completed a state-approved nurse aide training program in another state within the **last 24 months**.
- Must be currently listed on another state's registry in an **unexpired status and in good standing**.

### Reciprocity Application

> *Note: Out-of-state private duty employment does **not** qualify candidates for reciprocity.*

- Must have completed a state-approved nurse aide training program, passed competency testing, and be listed on another state's nurse aide registry.
- Must be currently listed on another state's registry in an unexpired status and in good standing, with no pending or substantial findings of abuse, neglect, exploitation, or misappropriation of resident/patient property on another state's registry.
- Must have been employed as a nurse aide for monetary pay (at least 8 hours performing nursing or nursing-related tasks delegated and supervised by an RN) in the **previous 24 consecutive months**.

## Registration Priority

- **Undocumented students** can begin registering the week before classes begin.

**Registration priority order:**
1. Currently enrolled students
2. Our CCP students
3. Other area CCP students
4. New students

- If a high school student graduates during one of our semesters (e.g., 1/29/24), they **cannot** register and attend classes until the **next semester**.
- We have **3 deregistration dates** — students are emailed about it; it shows on CRI and STAC as NP. To get back in, the student needs to pay and talk with an Ed Nav to re-register. **We will not be putting them back in** — the student and/or Ed Nav will do it. There should be a STRK comment about it.

## Foreign Language Placement

- We do **not** accept another institution's placement for a foreign language.
- However, students can advance to higher levels: a student can take the credit-by-exam tests for **SPA-111 & SPA-112**. If they earn **85% or higher**, Alyson Cochrane (lead instructor for Humanities) can progress the student to the next level.
- The student must complete the **Challenge Exam Request Form to Award Credit by Exam**, which includes a section for the student to explain their Spanish background and why they should test into an advanced course.
- A student can take the proficiency exam in any academic assistance center. It is best to have them contact **Begoña Buxens**, **Clyde Shafer**, or **Alyson Cochrane** for Spanish, or Dept. Chair **Ty Stumpf**.
- High school Spanish is **not** sufficient to place in higher-level Spanish classes.
