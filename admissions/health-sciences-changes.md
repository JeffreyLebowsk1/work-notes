# 🏥 Health Sciences Admissions Changes — 2025-2026 / 2026 Cohort

> **Note:** These changes apply to the admissions cycle for the **2026 cohort** unless otherwise specified. Administrative policy changes for this cycle supersede several sections of the published 2025–2026 Catalog. Always verify with the Health Sciences department or the current college catalog before advising students.

---

## Application Process — 2026 Cohort

**Effective:** 2026 Cohort

### What Changed
The limited enrollment application process is now **rolling and year-round** for most Health Sciences programs.

| Detail | Information |
|---|---|
| **Application Window** | Opens **November 1, 2025** — all required steps must be finalized by **May 15, 2026** |
| **Where to Apply** | Log into the **CCCC Portal** → **Success Hub** → click **"Limited Enrollment Application"** |
| **Selection Method** | **First-qualified, first-accepted** — seats are offered as students meet all eligibility criteria |
| **PTA Exception** | The **Physical Therapist Assistant (PTA)** program still uses a **ranked GPA system** for selection |
| **Notification** | Decisions are posted in the student's **Success Hub** |

### What You Need to Do Differently
Direct applicants to the Success Hub to apply. Emphasize the rolling nature of the process — students who complete their general education requirements earlier have a better chance of securing a seat.

## Minimum Eligibility Requirements

**Effective:** 2026 Cohort

Students should **not** submit the limited enrollment application until all of the following are met:

| Requirement | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Transcripts** | Official transcripts from high school and **all** previously attended colleges must be on file |
| **Academic Readiness** | Must be English and Math ready — no corequisite courses required |
| **Minimum GPA** | Cumulative **2.5 GPA** (or program-specific threshold — see table below) in program-specific general education courses |
| **Science Courses** | **"B" or better** in all required science courses (required for Full Acceptance; a "C" may qualify for Provisional Acceptance — see below) |
| **Other Gen Ed Courses** | **"C" or better** in all other required general education courses |

### Per-Program Minimum Admission GPA

The 2.5 GPA floor is a baseline; several programs require a higher threshold:

| Program | Minimum Admission GPA |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Dental Assisting** | 2.5 |
| **Dental Hygiene** | 3.0 |
| **Medical Sonography** | 3.0 |
| **Nursing (ADN)** | 2.8 |
| **Practical Nursing (PN)** | 2.5 |
| **Physical Therapist Assistant (PTA)** | 3.0 |
| **Veterinary Medical Technology (VMT)** | 2.5 |

> ⚠️ **Important policy change for 2026 cohort:** The "Alternate List" previously available for students with a 2.0–2.5 GPA has been **eliminated**. Students who do not meet the program-specific minimum GPA in the table above will not be accepted.

> **Note:** **Medical Assisting** and **Health Information Technology (HIT)** require a **2.0 GPA** for entry. These programs are **no longer subject to the rolling limited enrollment application window and seat allocation process** used by other Health Sciences programs. They still require ENG/MAT readiness and — for Medical Assisting — a current CNA transcript on file. See [Conflicts Pending Resolution](../updates/conflicts-pending.md) — CONFLICT 3 for details.

## Acceptance Status — Two-Tier System

**Effective:** 2026 Cohort

### What Changed
The traditional Alternate List has been eliminated. The single acceptance status has been replaced with two categories:

- **Fully Accepted** — Meets all criteria, including "B" or better in all required science courses. Student must **confirm their seat within two weeks** of the offer.
- **Provisionally Accepted** — Missing certain non-science general education courses, **or** holds a "C" in a required science course (rather than the "B" required for Full Acceptance). Seats are offered only if space remains after **May 15**.
  - ⚠️ **Exception:** **Dental Hygiene** and **Medical Sonography** do **not** offer Provisional Acceptance due to high demand.

### What You Need to Do Differently
Communicate clearly to applicants which status they have received and what steps or deadlines apply. Remind Fully Accepted students of the two-week confirmation window. Update SIS or tracking documents to distinguish between the two statuses.

### Provisional Seat Update Windows

While the general application window closes **May 15, 2026**, remaining seats for Provisionally Accepted students are offered during program-specific windows:

| Program | Provisional Seat Update Window |
| :--- | :--- |
| **ADN, PN, and Paramedic-to-RN** | May 18 – May 29, 2026 |
| **Physical Therapist Assistant (PTA)** | July 1 – July 15, 2026 |
| **Veterinary Medical Technology (VMT)** | July 15 – July 31, 2026 |
| **Dental Assisting** | Rolling until the mandatory orientation date |

> ⚠️ **Dental Hygiene** and **Medical Sonography** do **not** have a provisional window — seats in these programs are filled by Fully Accepted students only.

## TEAS Test — No Longer Required (Most Programs)

**Effective:** 2026 Cohort

### What Changed
The TEAS (Test of Essential Academic Skills) is **no longer required** for most Health Sciences programs.

### What You Need to Do Differently
Do not reject or hold Health Sciences applications solely for lack of TEAS scores. Before advising a student, verify directly with the relevant program department whether that specific program still has a TEAS requirement.

---

## TOEFL — No Longer Required for International Students

**Effective:** 2026 Cohort

### What Changed
The TOEFL requirement for international students has been **removed** for the 2026 Health Sciences admissions cycle. Successful completion of the required general education courses is considered sufficient evidence of English proficiency.

### What You Need to Do Differently
Do not reject or hold applications for international students solely for lack of TOEFL scores.

> ⚠️ **Catalog conflict:** The 2025–2026 Catalog pages for **Dental Hygiene** and **Health Information Technology** still list TOEFL as required for non-native English speakers. See [Conflicts Pending Resolution](../updates/conflicts-pending.md) — CONFLICT 2 — for details and resolution status.

---

## Statement of Good Standing — No Longer Required for Re-Applicants

**Effective:** 2026 Cohort

### What Changed
Re-applicants to Health Sciences programs are **no longer required** to submit a Statement of Good Standing.

### What You Need to Do Differently
Remove the Statement of Good Standing requirement from re-applicant checklists and communication templates for Health Sciences programs.

---

## PTA Prerequisites — Reduced from Eight to Four Courses

**Effective:** 2026 Cohort

### What Changed
Only **four** general education courses (instead of the previous eight) must be completed **prior to admission** to the **Physical Therapist Assistant (PTA)** program.

### What You Need to Do Differently
Update any advising materials, program sheets, or web content that states eight prerequisites are required before admission to PTA. Confirm with the PTA department which four courses are required pre-admission.

---

## Nursing Re-Entry — ATI Testing Course Required

**Effective:** Fall 2026

### What Changed
Students who exit the **ADN (Associate Degree Nursing)** or **PN (Practical Nursing)** programs must now complete an **ATI Testing self-paced nursing academic readiness course** as a prerequisite before they can re-enter the program.

### What You Need to Do Differently
Inform students who exit or are dismissed from ADN/PN of this new re-entry requirement. Ensure re-entry applicants submit documentation of ATI course completion before being processed for re-admission.

---

## Esthetics — Moving to First-Come, First-Serve with Waitlist

**Effective:** Fall 2026

### What Changed
Esthetics admissions will move from a **point-based selection system** to a **requirements + first-come, first-serve basis**, with a waitlist for applicants who meet requirements but don't secure a spot immediately.

### What You Need to Do Differently
Update Esthetics applicant communication to reflect the new admissions approach. Manage a waitlist accordingly starting with the Fall 2026 admissions cycle.

---

## VET Med Technology & Dental Hygiene — Updated Chemistry Requirements

**Effective:** Fall 2026

### What Changed
**Veterinary Medical Technology** and **Dental Hygiene** programs have updated their chemistry requirements. Students may now choose **one** of the following:
- CHM 130
- CHM 151
- BIO 180

### What You Need to Do Differently
Update program prerequisite checklists for Veterinary Medical Technology and Dental Hygiene to reflect the three-option chemistry requirement. Do not reject applications solely because a student completed BIO 180 instead of a chemistry course.

## Graduation Standards for Health Sciences Programs

Once admitted, students must maintain the following standards to remain in the program and graduate:

| Requirement | Standard |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Minimum Program GPA** | 2.0 |
| **Minimum Cumulative GPA** | 2.0 |
| **NUR prefix courses (ADN & PN)** | Final grade of **78 or higher** in every NUR course |
| **Transfer Degrees (AA, AS, AE)** | **"C" or better** in all required coursework |

> **Note:** Use **EVAL** or **PSPR** in Colleague to verify a student's program-specific GPA when confirming graduation eligibility. See [What-If EVAL/PSPR Guidance](../graduation/student-tracking/what-if-eval-pspr.md) for the full process.

## Staff Talking Points

Use the following when advising students about Health Sciences limited enrollment:

- *"You can apply directly through your **Success Hub** in the CCCC Portal. The window for the 2026 cohort is open until May 15."*
- *"Great news — we've removed the TEAS test and TOEFL requirements for this cycle to make the process more straightforward."*
- *"Admission is now primarily first-come, first-served for qualified students, so it's best to complete your general education courses with the required grades as soon as possible."*
- *"Most programs now use a rolling 'first-qualified, first-accepted' model. However, the **PTA program** still ranks applicants by GPA, making it a more competitive selection process."*
- *"If you are applying for **Dental Hygiene or Medical Sonography**, please be aware that these programs are highly competitive and do not offer provisional spots. You must meet all requirements fully to be considered."*
- *"If you are provisionally accepted, keep a close eye on your **Success Hub** during your program's specific update window in May or July to see if a seat becomes available."*

## Internal Staff Actions

### Verify Status in Colleague

Check the student's record on **IASU >> HSA** and **SPRO** screens to confirm transcript receipt and acceptance status ("Fully Accepted" or "Provisionally Accepted") before processing a limited enrollment application.

### Verify GPA Eligibility

Use the **EVAL** or **PSPR** mnemonics in Colleague to run a "what-if" evaluation and confirm the student's program-specific GPA against the minimum admission threshold. Ensure all external transcripts are received and evaluated via **IASU > HSA** before confirming eligibility. See [What-If EVAL/PSPR Guidance](../graduation/student-tracking/what-if-eval-pspr.md) for the step-by-step process.

### Veterinary Medical Technology — New Diploma Program (Fall 2026)

> 📝 A new **Diploma program in Veterinary Medical Technology** begins Fall 2026 with fewer stipulations than the AAS degree. When advising VMT-interested students, confirm whether they are applying to the AAS degree or the new Diploma program, as requirements differ.

### Medical Assisting & Health Information Technology — Program Status Conflict

> ⚠️ While the 2025–2026 Catalog lists **Medical Assisting** and **Health Information Technology** as limited enrollment programs, administrative updates specify they are **no longer treated as such**. Do **not** apply limited enrollment procedures to these programs until the status is confirmed. See [Conflicts Pending Resolution](../updates/conflicts-pending.md) — CONFLICT 3.

### GED Transcripts

If a student needs to order a GED/HSE transcript to meet the transcript requirement:

- Students must use **DiplomaSender** to order their transcript.
- Standard fee: **$20.00** per document.
- The transcript must be sent **directly to CCCC** at **admissions@cccc.edu**.
