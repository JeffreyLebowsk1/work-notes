# Guide to Applying to Central Carolina Community College

> This guide covers the full admissions process for prospective CCCC students. Internal staff can use it as a reference when advising applicants or answering questions.

---

## 1.0 Your Journey to Becoming a Cougar: The 5 Key Steps

The entire admissions process can be broken down into five essential steps:

1. **Choose Your Path & Apply** — Complete the online application to officially begin your journey.
2. **Establish NC Residency** — Complete a simple online form to determine your tuition rate.
3. **Submit Your Transcripts** — Send official academic records from high school and any colleges attended.
4. **Apply for Financial Aid** — Complete the FAFSA and explore scholarships.
5. **Get Ready for Classes** — After acceptance, meet with an advisor and register for the first semester.

---

## 2.0 Step 1: Submitting Your Application

The primary application route is through the **College Foundation of North Carolina (CFNC)** website.

### 2.1 The NC College Connect Program

Qualifying North Carolina public high school students may be eligible for a simplified application process called **NC College Connect**:

- Offers **direct admission** for students with a weighted GPA of **2.8 or higher**.
- Bypasses the traditional application process — no essays or recommendation letters needed.
- To claim a spot, students use the QR code on their official letter to log into the NC College Connect portal and complete the form.

> **Clarification:** Completing the form shows interest; it is **not** the final enrollment step. Students will receive direct communications about the next steps to officially enroll.

## 3.0 Step 2: Establishing NC Residency for Tuition Purposes

All applicants must complete a residency determination through the **North Carolina Residency Determination Service (RDS)** at [www.NCresidency.org](https://www.ncresidency.org). This is a mandatory step for every student applying to a public college in North Carolina.

### 3.1 How to Complete Your RDS Determination

1. Go to [www.NCresidency.org](https://www.ncresidency.org).
2. Complete the online interview (questions about residency history).
3. Receive your unique **Residency Certification Number (RCN)**.
4. Enter your RCN on your CCCC application.

### 3.2 Common Questions about Residency

| Question | Answer |
|---|---|
| Active-duty military, veteran, or military dependent? | May be eligible for in-state tuition through a military waiver. Contact the Registrar's Office for assistance. |
| Residency status changes or expires? | Request a reconsideration through your RDS account. If continuously enrolled, you can still register while status is being updated. |
| Need help with RDS? | Call (844) 319-3640 or email rdsinfo@ncresidency.org. |

## 4.0 Step 3: Sending Us Your Transcripts

Official transcripts are required for admission and financial aid. CCCC needs official transcripts from **all** high schools (or equivalent) and **every** college attended.

### 4.1 High School Transcripts

Request that your high school send the official, final transcript. North Carolina public high school graduates can often send for free through [CFNC.org](https://www.cfnc.org).

### 4.2 GED® or High School Equivalency Transcripts

If you earned a high school equivalency in North Carolina (GED® or HiSET), order your official transcript through **Diploma Sender** via the NC Community College System website.

### 4.3 Homeschool Transcripts

Homeschool applicants must provide:

- Proof of listing with the **N.C. Division of Non-Public Education (DNPE)**.
- A complete, final transcript that includes:
  - Course names and final grades
  - An unweighted cumulative GPA on a 4.0 scale
  - A grade scale (e.g., A = 90–100)
  - The exact graduation date (MM/DD/YYYY)
  - The homeschool administrator's signature

### 4.4 College Transcripts

Request official transcripts from all previously attended colleges. CCCC accepts official electronic transcripts sent directly from the institution or through services like **Parchment** and the **National Student Clearinghouse**.

> **Insight:** For prior college coursework to transfer to CCCC, the institution must be **regionally accredited**.

| Credits Typically Accepted | Credits Not Accepted |
|---|---|
| Institutions accredited by SACSCOC, HLC, MSCHE, NECHE, NWCCU, or WASC | Credits from institutions such as Miller-Motte, ITT Technical Institute, Study.com, Sophia Learning, and Penn Foster |

### 4.5 International (Foreign) Transcripts

- Must be evaluated and translated by a current member of [NACES](https://www.naces.org) or [AICE](https://www.aice-eval.org).
- The official, course-by-course evaluation must be sent **directly from the evaluation agency** to CCCC Admissions. If sent to the student first, it must remain in the original, sealed envelope.

> **Staff reference:** See [Foreign Students & Registration](./foreign-students-and-registration.md) for detailed credential evaluation service listings and visa-related procedures.

## 5.0 Find Your Path: A Guide for Every Student

### 5.1 High School Students (Career & College Promise — CCP)

The **Career & College Promise (CCP)** program offers tuition-free college credit for qualified high school juniors and seniors:

- **College Transfer (CT):** For students planning a four-year degree.
- **Career & Technical Education (CTE):** For students seeking a workforce credential, certificate, or diploma.

Students must work with their high school counselor to get approval and select a pathway.

### 5.2 Transfer Students

- Transfer up to **75%** of credits required for a CCCC program.
- Request official transcripts from all previously attended colleges.
- Credits are evaluated on a course-by-course basis.
- CCCC has transfer agreements with all **16 UNC System schools** and many NC independent colleges.

### 5.3 International Students (F-1 Visa)

Key steps:
1. Apply and be accepted by CCCC.
2. Receive **Form I-20** ("Certificate of Eligibility") from CCCC after acceptance.
3. Pay the required **I-901 SEVIS Fee** at [FMJFee.com](https://www.fmjfee.com).
4. Apply for a student visa at a U.S. embassy or consulate.
5. Arrive in the U.S. and report to the college to maintain student status.

> **Important:** International students on F-1, J-1, or J-2 visas **cannot** be admitted into Allied Health programs unless they have a valid **permanent resident card (Green Card)**.

> **Staff reference:** See [Foreign Students & Registration](./foreign-students-and-registration.md) for full details on visa types, Allied Health restrictions, registration priority, and foreign language placement.

### 5.4 Military & Veterans

- Active-duty service members, veterans, and their dependents may qualify for **in-state tuition** through a military waiver.
- VA benefits are available for students enrolled in a specific degree, diploma, or certificate program.
- Contact the **Veterans Affairs Coordinator** for personalized guidance.

### 5.5 Undocumented Students

- Undocumented immigrants are welcome to apply to CCCC.
- To enroll in curriculum (for-credit) courses, must have graduated from a U.S. high school.
- Under current state policy, undocumented students pay **out-of-state tuition** and are **not** eligible for Allied Health programs.

## 6.0 Applying to Health Sciences & Other Limited Enrollment Programs

Programs like Nursing, Dental Hygiene, Medical Sonography, and Veterinary Medical Technology are **limited enrollment** — they have a separate application process and a limited number of seats.

### 6.1 The Application Process

| Item | Details |
|---|---|
| Application Period (2026 cohort) | Opens **November 1, 2025** — all steps must be completed by **May 15, 2026** |
| How to Apply | After logging into the CCCC Portal, find the "Limited Enrollment Application" link in your Student Success Hub |
| Notification Timeline | Notifications begin **December 1, 2025**, on a rolling basis |

### 6.2 Key Changes for 2026 Applicants

- **No TEAS Test** — the TEAS test is no longer required for most of these programs.
- **New Acceptance Types** — "Fully Accepted" and "Provisionally Accepted" statuses give more students a clear path forward.
- **GPA is Key** — admission is based on GPA in required general education courses: **B or better in all science courses**, **C or better in all other general education courses**.

## 7.0 Step 4: Paying for College

### 7.1 Tuition & Fees (2025–2026 Rates)

| Credit Hours | Est. In-State Total | Est. Out-of-State Total |
|---|---|---|
| 3 | $367.25 | $943.25 |
| 6 | $595.25 | $1,747.25 |
| 12 (Full-Time) | $1,051.25 | $3,355.25 |
| 16+ (Full-Time) | $1,355.25 | $4,427.25 |

> Estimates do not include books, supplies, or course-specific fees. Rates subject to change.

### 7.2 Financial Aid (FAFSA)

Complete the **FAFSA** at [studentaid.gov](https://studentaid.gov). This determines eligibility for federal and state grants (funds that do not need to be repaid).

### 7.3 Scholarships & Promise Program

- **Central Carolina Promise (K14):** Up to two years of free in-state tuition and required fees for eligible recent graduates from **Chatham, Harnett, and Lee counties**.
- The **CCCC Foundation** offers additional scholarships.

### 7.4 Other Payment Options

| Option | Details |
|---|---|
| Payment Plans | Set up a deferred payment plan through **NELNET** |
| Employer Sponsorships | Employer tuition assistance may be available |
| Private Loans | CCCC accepts private educational loans from the lender of your choice |

## 8.0 You're In! What's Next?

1. **Set Up Student Accounts** — CCCC Portal, Success Hub, and CougarMail email.
2. **Meet Your Advisor** — Assigned advisor will help select classes and create an academic plan.
3. **Register for Classes** — Register for first semester with advisor guidance.
4. **Attend New Student Orientation** — Learn about campus resources and meet other new students.

|---|---|
| RDS (Residency Determination Service) | Mandatory online system for all NC public colleges to determine in-state vs. out-of-state tuition |
| FAFSA (Free Application for Federal Student Aid) | Official form for federal and state financial aid (grants, scholarships) |
| Limited Enrollment | Competitive programs (primarily health sciences) with special application requirements and limited seats |
| Prerequisite | A course that must be successfully completed before enrolling in a higher-level course |
| CCP (Career & College Promise) | Tuition-free dual enrollment program for qualified NC high school juniors and seniors |
| SEVIS | Student and Exchange Visitor Information System — federal tracking system for international students |
