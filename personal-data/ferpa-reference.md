# FERPA Quick Reference

> **FERPA = Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (20 U.S.C. § 1232g)**  
> Full reference: [law.cornell.edu — 20 U.S.C. § 1232g](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/20/1232g)

---

## Key Rights Under FERPA

Students (18+ or enrolled in post-secondary education) have the right to:
1. **Inspect and review** their education records within 45 days of a request.
2. **Request amendment** of education records they believe are inaccurate or misleading.
3. **Provide written consent** before the institution discloses personally identifiable information (PII) from their records.
4. **File a complaint** with the U.S. Department of Education for alleged FERPA violations.

> Note: At the post-secondary level, rights belong to the **student** — not the parent — regardless of the student's age or dependency status.

---

## What Are Education Records?

Records, files, documents, or other materials that:
- **Contain information directly related to a student**, AND
- **Are maintained by the institution** or by a party acting on behalf of the institution.

### Examples of Education Records
- Transcripts and grade reports
- Enrollment records
- Degree audit records
- Financial aid records
- Disciplinary files (with some exceptions)
- Advising notes (if maintained by the institution)

### What Is NOT an Education Record
- Sole possession records (personal notes not shared with others)
- Law enforcement unit records
- Employee records (for students who are also employees, if records relate only to employment)
- Medical/treatment records (if made only for treatment and not accessible to others)
- Alumni records created after the student's attendance

---

## Directory Information

Directory information may be disclosed without consent **unless the student has opted out**.

Typical directory information categories (verify your institution's designation):
- Name
- Address (local/campus)
- Telephone number
- Email address
- Major field of study
- Dates of attendance
- Enrollment status (full-time/part-time)
- Degrees, honors, awards received
- Participation in officially recognized activities and sports

> **Always check if a student has a directory information hold (FERPA block) before releasing any information — even directory information.**

---

## Annual Notification

The institution must notify students annually of their FERPA rights. This is typically done through:
- Student handbook
- Course catalog
- Institutional website
- Direct email notification

---

## Exceptions to Consent Requirement

Disclosure without student consent is permitted in these situations:

| Exception | Description |
|---|---|
| School officials with legitimate educational interest | Faculty, staff, administrators who need information to do their job |
| Transfer institutions | Records sent to schools where the student seeks to enroll |
| Financial aid | Records necessary to determine/award aid |
| State and local authorities / audit | Authorized by state statute |
| Judicial orders / subpoenas | Must make reasonable effort to notify student first |
| Health or safety emergency | Disclose to protect student or others from serious threat |
| Directory information | Unless student has opted out |
| Parents of dependent students (tax dependent) | Parents must provide documentation of dependency (e.g., IRS tax return) |

## Common Scenarios & Guidance

| Scenario | Guidance |
|---|---|
| Parent calls asking about student's grades | **Do not disclose** without student's written consent or proof of tax dependency |
| Student asks for their own records | Allow access within 45 days; do not charge for first copy if requested |
| Employer verifies a student's degree | Verify enrollment/degree only if it is designated directory info AND student has no opt-out; otherwise require student-signed release |
| Law enforcement requests records | Require subpoena or court order; make reasonable effort to notify student; consult counsel if needed |
| Faculty member asks about a student's enrollment status | OK only if they have a legitimate educational interest |
| Media/press asks about a student | **Do not disclose** PII; refer to Public Affairs or legal counsel |
