# Personal Data Handling Guidelines

## FERPA Overview

FERPA protects the privacy of student education records. All Registrar's Office staff must understand and follow FERPA requirements. See the [FERPA Quick Reference](./ferpa-reference.md) for details.

**When in doubt, do not disclose. Consult a supervisor.**

---

## What Counts as Education Records

Education records include **any record maintained by the institution that is directly related to a student**:
- Transcripts and academic history
- Enrollment and registration records
- Degree audit records
- Graduation records
- Residency classification records
- Financial aid records (in coordination with FA office)
- Advising records maintained by the institution
- Disciplinary records (with FERPA protections)

**Not** education records:
- Your personal notes that are not shared with others and not accessible to students
- Law enforcement records
- Employment records (for student workers, if unrelated to educational role)

## Who Can Access Student Records

| Who | Access Level | Conditions |
|---|---|---|
| The student themselves | Full access to their own records | Must verify identity |
| Registrar's Office staff | Access necessary for job duties | Legitimate educational interest |
| Faculty / Advisors | Limited — what they need to do their job | Legitimate educational interest |
| Other campus offices | Limited to what is needed for their role | Legitimate educational interest |
| Parents | Only if student is a tax dependent AND parent provides proof | Student must be a legal dependent |
| Law enforcement | With subpoena or court order; or in emergency | Notify student if subpoena |
| Third parties | **Only with student's written FERPA release** | Verify form is current and specific |

## Disclosure Procedures

### For Verbal Requests (Phone or In Person)
1. **Never release PII to an unverified caller.** Always verify identity first.
2. Ask for: full name, student ID, date of birth, and/or last four of SSN (if required by your institution).
3. Check for a **FERPA hold / directory information opt-out** in SIS before releasing even directory information.
4. Release only what is specifically requested and authorized.
5. Document unusual or sensitive requests.

### For Written/Email Requests
1. Do not respond to requests for student PII via unverified email.
2. If the request includes a signed FERPA release form, verify:
   - Student's signature
   - Scope of release (what may be disclosed)
   - Recipient is specifically named
   - Expiration date (if any)
3. Process the request and document it.

### For Requests Requiring a FERPA Release
1. Direct the requester to obtain a signed FERPA release from the student.
2. Once received, verify the form is complete and current.
3. Fulfill only the specific disclosure authorized.
4. File a copy of the release.

## Data Security Practices

- **Lock your screen** whenever you step away from your computer.
- **Never leave student records visible** on your screen in a public area.
- **Do not email student PII** (SSN, full DOB, grades) in plain text. Use encrypted/secure methods.
- **Print carefully** — retrieve printed student records immediately; do not leave on shared printers.
- **Shred** any printed student records when no longer needed.
- **Do not store student records** on personal devices or unapproved cloud services.
- **Strong passwords** — follow institutional IT password policy. Do not share credentials.
- Keep SIS sessions logged out when not in use.

---

## Reporting a Breach

If you suspect a data breach (records accessed without authorization, records sent to wrong person, lost/stolen device with student data, etc.):

1. **Do not delay.** Report immediately to your supervisor and the IT Security / Data Privacy Officer.
2. Document what happened: what data, how many students, when, and how you discovered it.
3. Do **not** attempt to investigate or cover up the incident.
4. Follow institutional incident response procedure: <!-- link to incident response plan -->
5. The institution is required to notify affected students under applicable law.
6. Cooperate fully with IT and legal counsel during the investigation.
