# Data Requests

> All data requests involving student records must comply with **FERPA**.  
> See [FERPA Quick Reference](./ferpa-reference.md) and [Handling Guidelines](./handling-guidelines.md).

## Internal Requests

Internal requests come from other offices or departments within the institution (e.g., Financial Aid, Advising, Athletics, Dean of Students).

1. Verify the requester's identity and institutional role.
2. Confirm that the requester has a **legitimate educational interest** in the student's data.
3. Provide only the minimum information necessary for their stated purpose.
4. Document the request: date, requester, purpose, and what was provided.
5. If uncertain about the legitimacy of the request, escalate to your supervisor before releasing.

---

## External Requests

External requests come from parties outside the institution (employers, other schools, government agencies, etc.).

1. **Do not release PII without a student-signed FERPA release form**, unless an exception applies (see [FERPA Reference](./ferpa-reference.md)).
2. Verify the FERPA release is current, complete, and specific.
3. Confirm the scope: what may be disclosed, to whom, and for what purpose.
4. Provide only what is authorized by the release.
5. Document the request and response.

### Verification Requests (Degree / Enrollment)
- Many employers and agencies use the **National Student Clearinghouse** for enrollment/degree verification.
- Direct external parties to [studentclearinghouse.org](https://www.studentclearinghouse.org) whenever possible.
- If handling directly: release only degree and enrollment status if it qualifies as directory information AND no opt-out is in place.

## Subpoenas & Legal Requests

1. **Do not respond immediately.** Notify your supervisor and legal counsel.
2. Review the subpoena/court order carefully — confirm it is valid and legally served.
3. **Make a reasonable effort to notify the student** before complying (unless the order specifically prohibits notification).
4. Respond within the required timeframe only after legal review.
5. Provide only the records specifically identified in the subpoena.
6. Document all steps taken.

> **Law enforcement requests without a court order** — generally do **not** disclose without a court order or signed student release, except in genuine health/safety emergencies. Consult counsel.

## Student Requests for Their Own Records

Students have the right under FERPA to inspect and review their own education records.

1. Student submits a written request identifying the records they wish to inspect.
2. Verify student identity (photo ID or portal login).
3. Provide access within **45 days** of the request (FERPA requirement).
4. Student may review records in person (supervised) or receive a copy.
5. **Costs:** The institution may charge a reasonable fee for copies, but **cannot charge a fee in a way that prevents the student from exercising their right**.
6. If the student believes a record is inaccurate or misleading, they may submit a written request for amendment:
   - Review the request.
   - Notify the student of the decision.
   - If amendment is denied, inform the student of their right to a formal hearing.
