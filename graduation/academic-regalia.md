# **A Symbol of Achievement and Tradition**

Academic regalia has a rich history dating back to medieval Europe, where scholars and clergy wore robes as a symbol of their learned status. Over time, these robes evolved into the standardized  
academic attire we see today. Today, academic regalia are worn with pride during graduation  
ceremonies as a symbol of academic achievement and scholarly tradition.

The colors and design of academic regalia are steeped in symbolism. Different colors represent  
different academic disciplines, while the style of the hood and other accessories may denote the level of the degree being conferred.  
For example:

* Black is the most common color for academic robes and symbolizes tradition, formality, and respect for the occasion.  
* Blue often represents degrees in philosophy (Ph.D.), and is sometimes used for gowns worn by graduates of other disciplines as well.  
* Light blue is often used to represent degrees in education or pedagogy.  
* White can symbolize a variety of disciplines, including arts and humanities.  
* Gold or yellow commonly represents degrees in the sciences, including mathematics,  
* engineering, and the physical sciences.  
* Green is often associated with degrees in medicine, health sciences, and veterinary medicine.  
* Purple is commonly used for degrees in law, theology, and divinity.  
* Red is often associated with degrees in fine arts, including music, theater, and visual arts.  
* Brown is sometimes used to represent degrees in architecture or other design-related fields.  
* Orange may be used for degrees in engineering or other technical disciplines.

For bachelor’s degrees, the gown typically has pointed sleeves. Master’s gowns have oblong sleeves with an opening near the wrist for the hands. Doctoral gowns often feature bell-shaped sleeves and may have velvet trim.

The hood is another important component of academic regalia, and its size, shape, and colors  
indicate the degree being conferred and the academic institution.
