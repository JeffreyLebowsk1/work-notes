# Strategic Communications Plan: Final-Term Student Graduation Application Campaign

## Introduction

This document outlines the strategic communications plan for Central Carolina Community College's Graduation Application campaign. The plan's purpose is to ensure all eligible students in their final term are aware of the mandatory application requirement and are equipped to successfully submit their application by the midterm deadline. A proactive, multi-channel approach is essential to prevent unnecessary delays in credential conferral and support student success.

---

## 1.0 Campaign Goal & Success Metrics

This section defines the campaign's ultimate purpose and establishes the clear, measurable criteria for its success.

### 1.1 Overarching Goal

To achieve a maximum application rate among all eligible final-term students by proactively informing them of the non-automatic graduation process and guiding them to a timely submission.

### 1.2 Key Success Metrics

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| **Awareness** | All students enrolled in designated capstone or 200-level courses are exposed to the core message that "graduation is not automatic" at least once through institutional channels |
| **Action** | 98% application submission rate among all identified eligible final-term students by the official midterm deadline |

---

## 2.0 Target Audiences & Key Stakeholders

### 2.1 Primary Audience

Students enrolled at Central Carolina Community College who are projected to complete all program requirements for a degree, diploma, or certificate in the current semester.

### 2.2 Key Stakeholders: Campaign Implementers

| Stakeholder | Role |
|---|---|
| **Capstone / 200-Level Instructors** | Deploy broad, timely announcements to entire classes of students in programs that lead to a credential. Provides wide-reaching initial awareness and subsequent reminders. |
| **Faculty Advisors** | Direct, personalized email outreach to students identified as likely final-term candidates. Provides a critical one-on-one touchpoint for confirmation and guidance. |

> **Strategy:** Instructors provide broad "air cover" for general awareness; faculty advisors execute the targeted "ground support" necessary for direct student conversion.

---

## 3.0 Core Messaging Framework

### 3.1 Central Theme

> **Graduation is not automatic; students must proactively apply.**

This message must be present in **all** communications.

### 3.2 Key Supporting Points

- The Graduation Application must be submitted via the **Success Hub**, accessible through the **CCCC Portal**.
- A **separate application** is required for each credential a student expects to earn (e.g., a degree and a certificate).
- Students must meet with their **Faculty Advisor** to confirm they are on track to complete all program requirements.
- Minimum academic requirements: **2.0 cumulative GPA** and **2.0 GPA in the program of study**.
- Failure to apply by the midterm deadline can result in a **delay of graduation** to a future academic term.

### 3.3 Primary Call to Action

All communications will drive students toward completing these three steps:

1. **Confirm Eligibility** — Meet with your Faculty Advisor to review your program requirements and confirm this is your final semester.
2. **Access the System** — Log in to the CCCC Portal and open the Success Hub.
3. **Submit Application** — Complete and submit the Graduation Application for each credential you intend to earn by the midterm deadline.

---

## 4.0 Multi-Channel Execution & Timeline

The campaign operates on a dual-track engagement strategy: a broad **awareness track** driven by instructors and a targeted **conversion track** driven by faculty advisors.

| Phase | Timing | Channel & Stakeholder | Strategic Action & Goal |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Awareness** | Week 1 | Instructor: Blackboard Announcement & In-Class Mention | Make students aware that graduation is not automatic and an application is required |
| **Direct Outreach** | Early in Term | Faculty Advisor: Personalized Email | Directly reach students identified as likely final-term candidates and prompt them to confirm their status |
| **Nudge** | Weeks 3–4 | Instructor: Blackboard Announcement | Nudge likely graduates to check their status with an advisor and apply if they have not already done so |
| **Final Push** | 1 Week Before Midterm | Instructor: Blackboard Announcement & Email; Faculty Advisor: Individual Outreach | Create urgency and ensure all remaining final-term students submit their application by the hard deadline |
