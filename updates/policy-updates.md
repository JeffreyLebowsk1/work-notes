## ADN Program — Humanities/Fine Arts Elective Pick List

**Category:** Policy
**Affects:** Curriculum, Advising, ADN Degree Audit (A45110)

### What Changed
Clarified that the ADN program (**A45110**) uses a specific approved pick list for its 5th-semester Humanities/Fine Arts elective — narrower than the general AAS elective options. Religion courses (REL 110, 211, 212) do **not** fulfill this requirement for ADN students.

### What You Need to Do Differently
- Advise ADN students to choose their Humanities elective only from the approved pick list (ART 111, ART 114, ART 115, HUM 115, MUS 110, MUS 112, or PHI 240).
- During EVAL, do not force a Religion course into the Humanities slot without written approval from the Nursing Department Chair (Dr. Barbara Campbell, Advisor #466734) and a Course Substitution Form on file.
- Confirm all ADN general education courses are completed with a **C or better**; NUR prefix courses require a **B (78 or higher)**.

### Links / References
See [ADN Program Requirements](../admissions/adn-program-requirements.md) for the full pick list and EVAL guidance.

## 2025-2026 — Course Repeat Policy Updated

**Category:** Policy
**Affects:** Student Records, Registration, Academic Departments

### What Changed
- A student seeking a **third attempt** of a course must first meet with the subject department chair or their designee.
- A **fourth attempt** requires a meeting with the dean.
- No student may attempt a course **more than four times**.

### What You Need to Do Differently
Before registering a student for a 3rd or 4th attempt, confirm that the required meeting has occurred and document it. Block registration for any student who has already attempted a course four times.

## November 2025 — Multiple Credentials Policy Reversed

**Category:** Policy
**Affects:** Graduation, Student Records

### What Changed
Effective **November 2025**, the previous policy of awarding only the highest credential is reversed. Students are now eligible to graduate and receive credentials from **all completed programs** (degree, diploma, and certificate), provided they apply for each one separately.

### Why It Changed
Policy change announced November 2025.

### What You Need to Do Differently
When auditing graduating students, check whether they qualify for credentials across multiple programs and ensure they have submitted an application for each. Do not automatically award only the highest credential.

### Links / References
2025-2026 College Catalog; Administrative announcement November 2025

## Fall 2025 — Course Expiration Policy Narrowed

**Category:** Policy
**Affects:** Student Records, Graduation, Advising

### What Changed
The general **five-year time limit on completed courses no longer applies** to most programs. Course expiration dates (time limits) now apply **only** to specific specialized Allied Health courses in the following programs:
- Dental
- Health Information Technology
- Medical Assisting
- Nursing
- Physical Therapy
- Medical Sonography
- Veterinary Medical Technology

### What You Need to Do Differently
Do not apply a five-year expiration to courses outside these Allied Health programs. When evaluating a student's degree requirements, only flag course expiration for the programs listed above.

## Fall 2025 — Academic Probation Renamed "Academic Warning"; Duration Extended

**Category:** Policy
**Affects:** Student Records, Advising, Financial Aid

### What Changed
- **Academic Probation** is now officially called **Academic Warning**.
- The maximum consecutive semesters a student may remain on Academic Warning before suspension is extended from **two** to **three** consecutive semesters with a term GPA below 2.0.

### What You Need to Do Differently
Use the term "Academic Warning" in all student communications, records, and reports — do not use "Academic Probation." Update any letter templates, SIS codes, or reports that used the old terminology. Be aware that the suspension threshold is now three consecutive low-GPA terms, not two.
