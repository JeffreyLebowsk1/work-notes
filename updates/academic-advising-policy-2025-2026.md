## 1. Institutional Foundation and Manual Scope

This manual stands as the authoritative synthesis of the official Central Carolina Community College (CCCC) Catalog and internal ethical guidance. It is a mandatory standard of practice for all Onboarding and Advising (O&A) and Registrar staff. It ensures institutional consistency by aligning registrar procedures with student success strategies, and serves as the bridge between the rigorous requirements of the CCCC Catalog and the "Culture of Care" that defines our practice.

### Core Values in Advising

The CCCC mission to foster individual, community, and economic development is operationalized through five core values. Navigators must apply these values to every student interaction:

| Value | Description |
|---|---|
| **Compassion** | Recognizing the diverse challenges students face and responding with empathy and a tangible willingness to assist in navigating institutional barriers. |
| **Inclusion** | Respecting and appreciating the unique qualities of every student, ensuring that advising services are accessible and equitable for all. |
| **Integrity** | Maintaining high standards of honesty, ensuring students receive accurate information regarding academic standing and financial aid. |
| **Opportunity** | Purposefully creating and protecting pathways that lead to "achievable dreams." |
| **Courage** | Inspiring students to take risks and move forward in the face of uncertainty while Navigators advocate for student needs against administrative inertia. |

## 2. Professional Standards for Ethical Advising

Ethical decision-making in academic advising requires a strategic balance between strict adherence to institutional policy and the individual needs of the student. Navigators often operate in "gray areas" where enrollment timing and program selection significantly impact financial aid eligibility.

### Ethical Decision Matrix

| Category | Commonly Accepted Situations | Questionable Situations |
|---|---|---|
| **Program Integrity** | Adding a second major (e.g., Associate in Science) to cover a prerequisite like MAT 171 (Pre-Calculus) for an AE student. | Adding a program (e.g., a Health Science major) solely to make a specific elective eligible for Financial Aid with no intent to pursue the field. |
| **Credit Maximization** | Double majoring to ensure all courses in a semester are "in-program" for Title IV coverage. | Adding a second transfer degree (AS/AE) to a completed AA just to stay aid-eligible for random math/science courses. |

### Golden Rules for Navigators

- **Prioritize Transparency:** Clearly explain the difference between a "Drop" and a "Withdrawal." Staff should utilize the RGCS screen in Datatel to verify Never Attends (NAs) that will eventually populate into STAC.
- **Advocate Through Evidence:** Use all available documentation — including "Sent" timestamps on emails — to support student requests.
- **Ensure Strategic Eligibility:** Only recommend program changes or double majors that genuinely align with the student's long-term academic plan.

> **Staff Directive:** Navigators must prevent "missed opportunities" regarding enrollment cycles. For example, many students are funneled into 2nd 8-week ACA 122 sections because 1st 8-week sections appear full, even though seats often open during the drop period. Proactive management of these mechanics is essential.

## 3. The Enrollment Lifecycle: Drops vs. Withdrawals

The distinction between a "Drop" and a "Withdrawal" is the most critical concept for a student to understand, as it dictates financial liability and the permanent academic record.

### Drop vs. Withdrawal Operational Guide

| Feature | Drop (Before/On 10% Point) | Withdrawal (After 10% to 75% Point) |
|---|---|---|
| **Refund Percentage** | 100% Total (75% immediate / 25% post-census) | No Refund (except for specific hardship/death) |
| **Grade Impact** | No grade recorded; removed from roll. | "W" recorded; no impact on GPA. |
| **Financial Aid** | Does not count as attempted hours. | Counts as attempted hours; impacts SAP. |

### Staggered Refunds and Communication

A Main Point of Contention (MPC) exists regarding the timing of refunds during the drop period. While a drop results in a 100% refund, the disbursement is staggered if the student has not yet had "Never Attend" (NA) status processed. In these cases:
- **75%** is issued immediately.
- **25%** is issued only after Census NAs are fully processed.

> **Staff Directive:** Students must be told to stay out of the class for a Drop to remain valid. This is different from a Withdrawal, where students should continue attending until the request is official. Always verify the "Sent" date of a student's request; if a student sends an email before a deadline, it must be honored based on that timestamp, regardless of when it is processed.

## 4. Financial Aid Integration and SAP Management

The strategic link between academic progress and Title IV funding is governed by Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP).

### The Three Pillars of SAP

1. **Qualitative (GPA):** Students must maintain a minimum cumulative GPA of 2.0.
2. **Quantitative (Pace):** Students must successfully complete at least 67% of all cumulative credit hours attempted.
3. **Maximum Timeframe (150%):** Students must complete their program within 150% of the published program length.

### Selected Grades Impact Guide

| Grade | Meaning | Affects GPA? | Affects Completion? | The "So What?" |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| W | Withdrew | No | Yes | Excessive "W"s lead to aid suspension via Pace. |
| WF | Withdrawal/Failing | Yes | Yes | Counts as an "F"; double negative impact. |
| I | Incomplete | Yes | Yes | Treated as an "F" until removed; see Midterm Rule below. |
| AU | Audit | No | No | Not eligible for Financial Aid coverage. |
| CE | Credit by Exam | No | Yes | Counts as attempted/earned; not aid eligible. |

> **Staff Directive:** If the instructor fails to submit a grade change for an "I" (Incomplete) by the midterm of the next semester, the grade converts to a permanent "F." Furthermore, financial aid does not perform "grade replacements" for repeats; all attempts count toward SAP.

## 5. Academic Sanctions and Student Recovery

Academic sanctions are intended as intervention tools rather than punishment, designed to help students regain their academic footing.

### The Sanctions Hierarchy

1. **Academic Warning:** Triggered when a term GPA falls below 2.0. Requires a meeting with an Education Navigator and enrollment in ACA 085 (Academic Recovery).
2. **Extended Academic Warning:** Triggered after a second consecutive term below 2.0.
   - Credit Hour Cap: Tailored to the student's academic plan.
   - Bi-Weekly Meetings: Mandatory check-ins with a Navigator.
   - Required Engagement: Mandatory use of the Academic Assistance Center or Library.
3. **Academic Suspension:** Occurs after the third term of unsatisfactory progress. Students are suspended for one semester.

### The Success Plan and Forgiveness

- **Readmission after suspension** requires a Success Plan approved by both the Navigator and the Academic Advisor. The student must meet with their Navigator at least three times during their return semester.
- **Academic Forgiveness** provides a one-time "clean slate" for students away for 36 consecutive months. Eligible students must complete 12 hours at a 100-level or higher with a 2.0 GPA. The Navigator must connect the student to the Dean of Student Onboarding and Success and the Dean of Health Sciences for final approval. *(Note: Confirm the appropriate approval authority for students outside Health Sciences programs.)*

## 6. Registrar Procedures and Documentation Standards

Accurate record-keeping is vital for institutional integrity. All staff must operate within the legal protections of FERPA. See also: [FERPA Quick Reference](../personal-data/ferpa-reference.md).

### Transcript Request Guide

| Feature | Official Transcript | Unofficial Transcript |
|---|---|---|
| **Request Method** | Online (Parchment) or In-Person | Self-Service Portal |
| **Fee** | $3.75 (Online) / $5.00 (In-Person) | Free |
| **Format** | Secure PDF or Sealed Paper | Web View / Printed Copy |

> **Staff Directive:** Verify identity via photo ID or Student ID number before discussing any student information.

### FERPA: Directory Information Checklist

The following may be shared without consent unless a privacy block is on file:
- Student Name and County of Residence
- Academic Major and Enrollment Periods
- Hours Earned, Degrees Awarded, and Awards/Honors Received

### Office Referral Protocol

| Office | Refer For |
|---|---|
| **Financial Aid** | Award amounts, FAFSA issues, and SAP appeal status. |
| **Veterans Affairs (VA)** | GI Bill® certification. Note: Navigators should refer all GI Bill questions to the VA Coordinator. |
| **Business Office** | Tuition payments, parking fines, and refund processing. |
| **Registrar** | Transcript evaluation, graduation applications, and FERPA blocks. |

## 7. Operational Appendices and Quick Reference

### Glossary of Critical Terms

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Census Date** | The 10% point of a course; the deadline for drops. |
| **Double Major** | Pursuing two degrees simultaneously via a "Change of Program" form. |
| **ACA Waiver** | A waiver for students with prior degrees or 18+ hours of transfer credit. |
| **Quality Points** | The values used for GPA calculation: A=4, B=3, C=2, D=1, F=0. |

### Navigator's Checklist for Program Changes

- [ ] **Verify Catalog of Record:** Ensure the student is moved to the catalog version current at the time of the change.
- [ ] **Form Completion:** Ensure the "Change of Program" form is signed and filed.
- [ ] **Financial Aid Review:** Verify the new program is aid-eligible.
- [ ] **Self-Service Update:** Update the success plan to reflect the new requirements.
